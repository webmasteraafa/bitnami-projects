Part 2: New Allergy-Friendly Foods for Kids

The Natural Products Expo West, a food and natural products industry trade show, celebrated its 30th anniversary at its recent expo in Anaheim, California. Featuring exhibits from over 1,700 companies, many of which accommodate the allergy community, the event showcased the latest in natural food and products.

This spring's expo attracted a record 56,000 industry attendees, including Kids With Food Allergies. KFA's representative tracked down the newest products to share with you—parents of kids with food allergies. Read the report to find out what new products are now available!

Beanitos Bean Chips

Company: Bean Brand Foods, www.beanitos.com

Description: Round snack chips made from all-natural non-GMO ingredients, with beans and whole grain rice at the top of the ingredient list. Available in two flavors: Black Bean Chips and Pinto Bean & Flax Chips. Beanitos are great with your favorite salsa or dip, as a hearty base for nachos, or right out of the bag as a delicious on-the-go snack that really satisfies without the empty calories of other chips.

Free of these allergens:

Milk
Egg

Wheat
Gluten
Peanut
Tree nut
Fish
Shellfish

Soy
Corn

Cross-Contamination Issues: Although these chips currently have the words “Always Corn Free” prominently featured on the front of the package, the back of the package warns that they are "made in a facility that may also use soy, dairy, seeds, wheat, corn, eggs, peanuts and tree nuts."

The company representatives explained that these allergens are currently in their shared facility, but on completely separate machinery. They hope to have their own allergen-free facility by the end of 2010.

Allergen Labeling Policy: Will always put warning if in same facility as any of the “top 8 allergens”.

Availability: Available online at Amazon.com. Also available at various natural products and grocery stores including Whole Foods Markets and Sprouts.


Blue Diamond Almond Butter

Company: Blue Diamond, www.bluediamond.com

Description: Almond Butter, available in six varieties: ReadySpread Creamy, ReadySpread Crunchy, ReadySpread with Honey, Homestyle Creamy, Homestyle Crunchy, Homestyle with Honey. The “ReadySpread” have a no-stir formula that includes palm oil, while the “Homestyle” varieties require stirring but contain no added oils. The honey variety is not vegan.

Free of these allergens:

Milk
Egg

Wheat
Gluten
Peanut
Tree nut
Fish
Shellfish

Soy

Cross-Contamination Issues: All Blue Diamond products are produced in peanut-free facilities. The labels on the almond butter line all clearly state "may contain other tree nuts." A company representative stated that although no other tree nuts are actually used in the facility, they include this warning because other tree nuts might be in with the almonds when they are delivered from the growers (some of which grow other tree nuts in addition to almonds). In addition, there are products that contain soy that are produced in the same facility, although on different production lines.

Allergen Labeling Policy: Will always put a warning if made in the same facility as something which contains peanuts, dairy or other tree nuts. The other “top 8 allergens” are not currently included in their labeling policy.

Availability: Available online at BlueDiamond.com and will soon be available at Amazon.com. In stores nationally at Whole Foods Markets and other natural foods stores; check the store locator for a location near you.

Creating Better Lives Today... One Child at a Time

Kids With Food Allergies is dedicated to creating better lives—today—for children with food allergies. As one of the largest organizations for families managing food allergies, KFA offers parents the latest information, helpful food and cooking advice, and a community of support to help families raise healthy, happy, well-nourished children. And because no parent should feel alone, KFA provides hope, comfort and friendship — 24 hours a day, seven days a week.
