Company:

Comments:
 For ages 11-64; contains same antigens as Daptacel®, however formulated with reduced quantities of Diphtheria Toxoid and Detoxified Pertussis Toxin.

The vial stopper is latex-free.

Influenza, inactivated, injectable (TIV)


Brand Name:
 Afluria® (2010-2011)
Company:
 CSL Limited
www.csl.com.au
Inactive Ingredients:
(per dose)
 Each 0.5 ml dose contains sodium chloride 4.1 mg, monobasic sodium phosphate 80 mcg, dibasic sodium phophate 300 mcg, monobasic potassium phophate 20 mcg, potassium chloride 20 mcg, and calcium chloride 1.5 mcg. From the manufacturing process, each dose may contain residual amounts of sodium taurodeoxycholate <10 ppm, ovalbumin <1 mcg, neomycin sulfate <0.2 pg, polymixin B <0.03 pg, and beta-propriolactone <25 ng.1
Vaccine-Production Media:
 Allantoic fluid of embryonated chicken eggs.
Contraindications To Use:
 Hypersensitivity to eggs, neomycin, or polymixin, or life-threatening reaction to previous influenza vaccine.1

The FDA includes the contraindication of vaccination of persons with a history of Guillain-Barre Syndrome (GBS), but it is not included in the ACIP recommendations. The ACIP believes that the benefits of influenza immunization justify annual vaccination, but cautions not to administer the vaccine within 6 weeks of GBS. 2(208), 5
Comments:
 The CDC has recommended that this particular vaccine not be routinely administered to children aged 6 months through 8 years.9

The multi-dose vial contains thimerosal as a preservative - each 0.5 ml dose contains 24.5 mcg of mercury. The single-dose syringe formulation is preservative-free.1

The rubber tip cap and plunger used for the single-dose syringes and the rubber stoppers used for the multi-dose vial contain no latex.1

Measles, Mumps, Rubella (MMR)


Brand Name:
M-M-R II®
Company:
Merck Vaccine Division
www.merckvaccines.com
800-525-2065
Inactive Ingredients:
(per dose)
Sorbitol, sodium phophate, sucrose, sodium chloride, hydrolyzed gelatin, recombinant human albumin, fetal bovine serum, neomycin, no preservative.
Vaccine-Production Media:
 Measles and mumps: Chicken embryo cell and Medium 199 with sucrose, phophate, glutamate, recombinant human albumin, fetal bovine serum, and neomycin.

Rubella: Human diploid lung fibroblasts, Minimum Essential Medium with fetal bovine serum, recombinant human albumin, neomycin, sorbital and hydrolyzed gelatin stabilizer.
Contraindications To Use:
 Hypersensitivity to any component of the vaccine, including gelatin; anaphylaxis to neomycin; acute febrile respiratory illnesses or other active febrile illness; active immunosuppressive therapy (except replacement corticosteroid therapy); blood dyscrasias, leukemia, lymphoma or other malignant neoplasms affecting bone marrow or lymphatic systems; primary and acquired immunodeficiency states; and family history of congenital or hereditary immunodeficiency.

The AAP has stated, "Most children with a history of anaphylactic reactions to eggs have no untoward reactions to measles or MMR vaccine. Persons are not at increased risk if they have egg allergies that are not anaphylactic, and they should be vaccinated in the usual manner. In addition, skin testing of egg-allergic children with vaccine has not been predictive of which children will have an immediate hypersensitivity reaction...Persons with allergies to chickens or chicken feathers are not at increased risk of reaction to the vaccine."1

Active untreated tuberculosis.2 (235)
Comments:
 Similar, but not exact vaccines are Priorix® (GlaxoSmithKline - Australia), Tresivac® (Serum Institute of India, Ltd), Trimovax® (Sanofi Pasteur - Europe).

Varicella (Chickenpox)


Brand Name:
Varivax®
Company:
Merck Vaccine Division
www.merckvaccines.com
800-525-2065
Inactive Ingredients:
(per dose)
Sucrose, hydrolyzed gelatin (porcine), sodium chloride, monosodium L-glutamate, sodium phosphate dibasic, potassium phosphate monobasic, potassium chloride, residual components of MRC-5 cells including DNA and protein; and trace quantities of sodium phosphate monobasic, EDTA, neomycin, and fetal bovine serum; no preservative.
Vaccine-Production Media:
 Human diploid cell culture.
Contraindications To Use:
 History of hypersensitivity to any component of the vaccine, including gelatin; history of anaphylactoid reaction to neomycin; individuals with blood dyscrasias, leukemia, lymphoma of any type, or other malignant neoplasms affecting the bone marrow or lymphatic system; individuals receiving immunosuppressive therapy, individuals with primary and acquired immunodeficiency states; family history of congenital or hereditary immunodeficiency; active untreated tuberculosis; and any febrile respiratory illness or other active febrile infection.1
Comments:
 Vaccination should be deferred for at least 5 months following blood or plasma transfusions, or administration of immune globulin.1

The ACIP recommendations state that neomycin allergy is usually of the contact dermatitis type, which is a delayed immune response rather than anaphylaxis. Therefore a history of contact dermatitis to neomycin is not a contraindication to receiving varicellar vaccines.5
